Where things stand - the one-minute read

sysdn03 is the EKS cluster running our customer-facing portal (Java/Tomcat) and its customers Postgres database, plus an AI workload and the usual platform components - 18 scanned assets in all.

The cluster is quiet in day-to-day terms, but it is carrying one sharp, unresolved risk: the customer portal runs a nine-year-old version of Apache Struts with seven weaponized remote-code-execution flaws. Today that risk materialized. An attacker exploited the portal, dumped the customers table, and stole the pod's AWS credentials - the whole chain in a 12-second window. Sysdig detected every step in real time; it was not blocked, so we treat customer data and those cloud credentials as exposed.

Beyond that incident, the standing picture is a large fixable-vulnerability backlog (89% of assets fail policy), concentrated in the portal image and the node's OS packages. None of it is exotic - it is patch debt with clear owners.

What we need from you
  1. Approve an emergency rebuild of portal off Struts 2.5.10 (the root cause). Treat it as actively exploitable until done.
  2. Authorize IAM credential rotation for the portal workload and a CloudTrail review of account 059797578166 since {{INCIDENT_TIME}}.
  3. Endorse three standing controls: enforce IMDSv2, run the container as non-root, and block pod egress to the metadata endpoint.
